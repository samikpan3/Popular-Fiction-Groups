This graph plots number of checkouts against book genres. I wanted to investigate among popular fiction books genres, which genres were most popular in Seattle Public Library. Therefore, Genre and Checkouts were placed in position axis. I also wanted to know if we could observe any difference in checked out books and genre across season. Previously, I had added this information by encoding season in facet. Now, through dropdown option, users can decide the season themselves. Because I have preserved total checkout against genres through opacity, users can now easily compare the ratio of book borrowed in that particular season against total books borrowed of that genre in 2022. Furthermore, I also added selection of genres as a radio bottons, that way, if a user wants to know the title of the books that were borrrowed in a particular season across specific genre they can do so. Previously, I could add title information, because it visually cluttered my graph, but now through interactive tools and tooltip (for title for book), users can know the most checked out book in summer season within romance genre.

I investigated the Fictionbooks checked out from Seattle Public Library for year 2022. I got this data from Seattle.gov Open data portal (https://data.seattle.gov/Community/Checkouts-by-Title/tmmm-ytt6) which keeps monthly count of Seattle Public Library checkouts by title for physical and electronic items.
